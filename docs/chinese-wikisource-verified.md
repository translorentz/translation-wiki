## Notes on Borderline Cases

### Confirmed with Caveats
- **Mingru Xue'an:** Julia Ching's "selected translation" covers only a portion of this massive 62-juan work. Confirmed because the translation is explicitly partial and the untranslated majority is very large.
- **Yuewei Caotang Biji:** Multiple partial translations exist but collectively cover less than 10% of ~1,200 stories. Confirmed because no translation approaches completeness.
- **Fenshu:** Handler-Spitz et al. translated selections from both Fenshu and Cangshu, but it is explicitly "selected writings." The untranslated majority of Fenshu remains large. Confirmed.

### Rejected with Notes
- **Yantie Lun:** Gale translated chs. 1-28 of 60 (~47%). This is borderline but substantial enough to reject.
- **Jinghua Yuan:** Lin Tai-yi's version covers only 33 of 100 chapters. Borderline, but a published translation from a major press (UC Press) exists.
- **Guanchang Xianxing Ji:** T.L. Yang's 2002 translation is abridged but published by HK University Press.
- **Baopuzi:** Inner Chapters have two complete translations (Ware 1966, DTC 2025). Outer Chapters (50 ch.) remain fully untranslated. Rejected because the most famous and studied part has complete translations.
- **Canglang Shihua:** Chen's complete translation is a dissertation, not a commercial book — but it IS a complete English translation available through ProQuest. Rejected.
- **Xu Xiake Youji:** Hargett 2025 is described as "most inclusive selection to date" — extensive but still not fully complete. Rejected because a major published translation now exists.
