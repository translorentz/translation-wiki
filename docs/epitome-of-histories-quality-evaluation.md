## Overview

The Epitome of Histories is a 12th-century Byzantine chronicle by John Zonaras covering world history from creation to 1118 CE. The text was processed from three volumes of the Dindorf critical edition, extracting Greek text while attempting to filter Latin translations and critical apparatus.

## Summary of Issues

### Issue Frequency by Type

| Issue Type | Frequency | Severity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Critical apparatus (manuscript sigla) | Very High (~15-25% of paragraphs) | Critical |
| Latin text/commentary | High (~5-10% of paragraphs) | Critical |
| FONTES citations | Medium (~1-2 per chapter) | Moderate |
| Section headers/marginalia | Medium (~3-5 per chapter) | Moderate |
| Fragmented sentences | Medium (~5% of paragraphs) | Moderate |
| Single-word/stub paragraphs | Low (~2-3% of paragraphs) | Minor |

### Patterns Observed

1. **Apparatus lines typically start with:**
   - Line numbers (e.g., "12", "22")
   - Square brackets with variants: `]`
   - Manuscript sigla: A, B, C, P, W, R, E, D
   - Editor names: Wolfius, Ducange, Regiis

2. **Latin markers:**
   - "ita", "sic", "om", "add", "infra", "sed v. ad"
   - "cod.", "codex", "codices"
   - "Vaticanum", "excerptum"

3. **FONTES pattern:**
   - Always starts with `ἘΌΝΤΕΒ` or `FONTES`
   - Followed by chapter references

## Overall Grade: D (Significant Issues - Needs Cleaning Before Translation)

### Rationale

The processed text contains approximately 15-25% contaminated paragraphs across all three volumes. This contamination includes:

- Critical apparatus (manuscript variants, sigla)
- Latin translation text and editorial commentary
- FONTES source citations
- Section headers from the critical edition
- Editor names and references (Wolfius, Ducange, etc.)

This level of contamination will cause significant problems for AI translation:
1. The translation model will attempt to translate Latin text as Greek
2. Manuscript sigla will produce nonsensical translations
3. FONTES citations will clutter the translated text
4. The paragraph structure is broken in many places

---

## Recommendations

### DO NOT proceed with translation in current state.

### Required Cleaning Steps:

1. **Filter Apparatus Lines**
   - Remove paragraphs that are primarily manuscript sigla
   - Pattern: Lines containing `] ita`, `] sic`, manuscript letters (A, B, C, P, W) followed by variants
   - Pattern: Lines starting with numbers followed by editorial content

2. **Remove FONTES Citations**
   - Filter lines matching `/^[ΕF]ONTES\./` or `/ἘΌΝΤΕΒ/`

3. **Strip Latin Content**
   - Remove lines containing Latin editorial terms: "ita", "sic etiam", "cod.", "Vaticanum", "excerptum"
   - Remove lines with editor names: "Wolfius", "Ducange", "Regiis"

4. **Fix Paragraph Boundaries**
   - Merge stub paragraphs (single words) with adjacent text
   - Detect and join sentences split across paragraphs

5. **Clean Section Headers**
   - Remove or relocate marginal section headers that leaked into text

### Estimated Effort

A reprocessing script with proper filtering rules would need to:
- Identify and remove ~1,500-2,200 contaminated paragraphs
- Merge ~200-400 fragmented paragraphs
- Review results for false positives

### Alternative: Manual Review

Given the complexity of the apparatus notation, a semi-automated approach may work better:
1. Flag suspicious paragraphs automatically
2. Human reviewer confirms/rejects each flag
3. Reprocess with reviewed filter list

---

## Conclusion

The Epitome of Histories processing has extracted the Greek text but failed to adequately filter the critical apparatus that accompanies the Dindorf edition. The result is a text that mixes genuine historical narrative with editorial apparatus at a ratio that makes it unsuitable for translation without significant cleanup.

**Recommendation**: Return to processing stage and implement robust apparatus filtering before proceeding with translation.
